The world of aviation is vast and interconnected, with over 5,000 airlines and 40,000 airports spanning the globe. From major international carriers and bustling mega-hubs to regional airlines and small airstrips, the aviation industry plays a crucial role in connecting people, places, and cultures. Whether you're planning your next trip, tracking flights, or simply exploring the fascinating world of aviation, this page is your one-stop resource for everything airlines and airports.

Did You Know?

The largest airline by fleet size is American Airlines, operating over 900 aircraft.
The busiest airport by passenger traffic is Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL), handling more than 100 million travelers annually.
The longest non-stop commercial flight is operated by Singapore Airlines, flying from Singapore to New York, covering over 9,500 miles.
The highest-altitude commercial airport is Daocheng Yading Airport (DCY) in China, sitting at 14,472 feet above sea level.
The world's oldest operating airport is College Park Airport (CGS) in Maryland, USA, established in 1909.

Airline Alliances

The three main airline alliances—Star Alliance, Oneworld, and SkyTeam—connect major airlines worldwide, offering passengers seamless travel benefits. Star Alliance, the largest, includes airlines like United, Lufthansa, and Singapore Airlines, covering over 1,300 destinations. Oneworld, featuring carriers like American Airlines, British Airways, and Qatar Airways, provides premium services and extensive global connectivity. SkyTeam, with members such as Delta, Air France-KLM, and Korean Air, focuses on streamlined international travel and shared airport lounges. These alliances enhance frequent flyer perks, simplify transfers, and expand route options for travelers globally.
